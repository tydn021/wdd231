Harry Oliver

  • Birth: April 17th, 1888
  • Death: July 4, 1973

The man most responsible for creating the Storybook Style and getting it going, Harry Oliver was born in 1888 in Hastings, Minnesota. Harry Oliver had a very rewarding career as a movie set painter and would eventually move up to become a world class Art Director for films. He also won two academy awards for his work on "7th Heaven" and "Street Angel". The thing he brought to films was a disdain for straight lines nad firmly believed the movie sets he helped build should have an organic sagging vibe to them making them like fairy tales. He would bring this to his later designs for houses and buildings across Los Angeles, cementing the look of Storybook Style. He would eventually die in Woodland Hills, California after leading a fulfilling life

Hugh Comstock

  • Birth: April 17th, 1893
  • Death: June 21st, 1950

If you read through the information on the houses presented on this site, you will notice a name come up repeatedly; Hugh Comstock. A man from humble origins being born in Evanston, Illinois on a farm, his family would sell the farm and move to California. Hugh Comstock would eventually find himself serving in Word War 1 and returning to start a life with his wife Mayta. She was a toy maker and built popular Dollhouses. The two of them would end up in Carmel By The Sea, and desiring a house like her dollhouses, asked Hugh Comstock who had a background in construction to build her a house like her dollhouses. The results would prove very popular with the denizens of Carmel and so a demand for his whimsical houses which now dot the landscape of Carmel By The Sea to this day. Sadly in 1950, Hugh Comstock died from a tragic fall. His legacy in Carmel still stands to this day.

Ben Sherwood

  • Birth: May 17, 1889
  • Death: September (?), 1968

Ben Sherwood was born in 1889 and would prove to be wildly influential on the Storybook Style scene. Having had a very prolific career in Los Angeles, he would build several Storybook Style pieces. In 1931 he would build the Griffith Park Manor which features fairy-tale cottages. Walt Disney's Animators actually lived in these houses and living in Sherwood's cottages served as a major inspiration for the movie "Snow WHite and the Seven Dwarves".
